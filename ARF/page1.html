Now if it is on land, under sun, it will start getting evaporated (it is tendency of water to get evaporated because of heat).

Water makes different sounds as per it's temperature.

The hot water and cold water, both produces a different sound when poured. Ever noticed that? I think you haven't. But here I will tell you the reason and you can experiment it at home. Basically, the heat changes the thickness or viscosity of the water and that results in the pitch of the sound that water produces. Actually the heat comes from the molecules of water moving. In hot water molecules moves faster, the thickness is less and makes slightly low-pitched sound whereas cold water is thicker a makes a bit high-pitched sound.

Peanuts aren’t technically nuts

They’re legumes. According to Merriam-Webster, a nut is only a nut if it’s “a hard-shelled dry fruit or seed with a separable rind or shell and interior kernel.” That means walnuts, almonds, cashews, and pistachios aren’t nuts either. They’re seeds.

Most Disney characters wear gloves to keep animation simple

Walt Disney might have been the first to put gloves on his characters, as seen in 1929's The Opry House starring Mickey Mouse. In addition to being easier to animate, there's another reason Disney opted for gloves: “We didn’t want him to have mouse hands because he was supposed to be more human,” Disney told his biographer in 1957.

There were active volcanoes on the moon when dinosaurs were alive

Most of the volcanoes probably stopped one billion years ago, but new NASA findings suggest there might still have been active lava flow 100 million years ago, when dinosaurs were still roaming.

Mercedes invented a car controlled by joystick

The joystick in the 1966 Mercedes F200 showcase car controlled speed and direction, replacing both the steering wheel and pedals. The car could also sense which side the driver was sitting in, so someone could control it from the passenger seat.
